# Background and Overview

LastMile is a store that sells office supplies and furnishings nationwide across the United States. The company has significant amounts of data in sales. This project analyzes and synthesizes this data in order to uncover critical insights that will provide LastMile's commercial success.

Insights and recommendations are provided on the following key areas:

- Sales Trend Analysis: Evaluation of sales trends 
- Product Level Performance: an analysis of LastMiles various products, understanding their impact on sales and profit
- Regional Comparisons: an evaluation of sales and orders by region

An interactive Tableau dashboard can be downloaded [here](https://public.tableau.com/views/LastMileStoreSalesDashboard/Dashboard1?:language=en-US&:sid=&:redirect=auth&:display_count=n&:origin=viz_share_link)

# Executive Summary

## Overview of Findings

The LastMile Store Sales Analysis shows total sales of $733,215 and profit of $93,439 across 693 customers. Sales performance varies by region and product category, with strong concentration in a few key states and high-value items. California leads all states $146,388, followed by New York at $93,923, and Washington at $65,540. Most remaining states fall below $30,000 in sales, showing a large gap between top and mid-tier markets. Several states, including Alaska, Hawaii, Maine, and Wyoming, recorded $0 in sales. This suggests either no recorded transactions or missing data. Customer activity shows that orders are widely distributed but heavily concentrated in high-population states, indicating that both demand and regional market size strongly influence total revenue. Product categories also show large differences, with phones generating $105,341 while the lowest category, Fasteners, generated only $858. These findings highlight where the company is performing well and where growth opportunities exist.

## Sales Trends:

- Monthly sales increased steadily throughout the year, peaking in November at $118,448.
- The lowest month was February with $20,301, showing early-year slow performance.
- Sales sharply rise from August to November, indicating a strong seasonal trend.
- High-sales states are mostly coastal and urban, suggesting population density impacts revenue.
- States like California, New York, Florida, and Michigan consistently outperform smaller markets.
  
## Product Performance:

- Phones are the highest-selling product with $105,341, well above the average of $43,130.
- Chairs follow with $95,554, showing strong demand for office furniture.
- Other strong performers include Binders ($72,788), Storage ($69,678), Copiers ($62,899), and Tables ($60,894).
- Mid-level products, such as Accessories ($55,946), Machines ($43,545), and Appliances ($42,927), perform near or slightly below average.
- Lower-performing items include Art ($8,863), Labels ($3,863), Envelopes ($3,379), and Fasteners ($858).
- The large gap between top and categories suggests customers prioritize tech and office essentials over small, stationary items.

# Recommendations

- Focus marketing and inventory on top-performing categories: Phones, Chairs, Binders, Storage, Copiers, and Tables.
- Expand mid-performing items into strong markets to raise their sales above the $43,130 average.
- Run promotions or bundles for low-selling categories to clear slow-moving inventory.
-  Strengthen presence in high-opportunity states such as California, New York, Washington, Florida, Pennsylvania, and Michigan.
-  Investigate states with $0 sales (Alaska, Hawaii, Maine, Wyoming) for possible supply chain issues or missing distribution.
-  Use targeted seasonal campaigns from August-November, when sales naturally peak.
